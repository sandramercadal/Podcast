What is a career break?

Many professionals decide to take an intentional break from their careers at some point. Whether by choice or circumstance, it often comes with a complex mix of emotions and implications.
The reasons for stepping away from work vary widely, including caregiving responsibilities, health issues, relocation, redundancy, or pursuing personal and professional development to name a few.

Returner Stories

Embracing the insights gained during your break can be a key to unlocking the next stage of your career ✨


Name: Sandra
Career Break Duration: 4.5 years
We really need to change the narrative around career breaks. In 2017, I stepped back from my career due to burnout, intending to take one year off. That turned into 4.5 years, and I’m grateful to have returned to a senior role in 2022, though I know many aren’t as fortunate.
The Journey:
My confidence waned, my network had dwindled, and I prepared myself to accept a role I was overqualified for just to return to work. I started job searching daily at 5:30 a.m! In 2021, I found Link Up London’s “ReLaunch” program, a lifeline for women wanting to return to work that helped me rebuild my confidence through peer support, coaching, and structured exercises.
Top tips:
✨ Reflect on your values: This is key!
✨ Listen: Podcasts offer practical advice and wisdom.
✨ Celebrate small wins: Recognise the progress.
✨ Friends & Family network: Share your goals with them.
✨ Persevere: Stay resilient — don’t give up! 🌈
